## Nonlinear Parker-Oldenburg Gravity Inversion with Lithosphere Flexure and Adaptive Spectral Filtering

-- This document is written by Marco Ligi

This work presents a nonlinear 2-dimensional gravity scheme based on the Parker-Oldenburg formulation, extended to include lithospheric flexure, spatially variable elastic thickness, Tikhonov regularization and adaptive spectral filtering. The method estimates Moho depth variations from Bouguer anomaly data using iterative Fourier-domain updates.

---

### Forward Problem

The gravitational anomaly generated by a variable $h(x,y)$ interface is expressed as:

$$
g(x,y) = 2\pi G \Delta \rho \, \mathcal{F}^{-1} [ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{|k|^{n-1}}{n!} \mathcal{F} \{ h^{n}(x,y) } ] e^{-|k|z_0} 
\qquad(1)
$$

Where $\mathcal{F}$ denotes the 2-dimensional Fourier transform.

---

### First-order Parker-Oldenburg Solution

The classic linearized solution in Fourier domain is:

$$
h^{(1)}(k) = - \frac{g(k) e^{|k|z_0}}{2\pi G \Delta \rho} 
\qquad(2)
$$

Defining $C = 2\pi G \Delta \rho$:

$$
h^{(1)}(k) = - \frac{g(k) e^{|k|z_0}}{C} 
\qquad(3)
$$

---

### Nonlinear Iterative Correction

The full nonlinear solution is obtained iteratively:

$$
h^{(n)}(k) = h^{(1)}(k) - \sum_{m=2}^{\infty} \frac{|k|^{m-1}}{m!} \mathcal{F} { h^{(n-1)}(x,y)^{m} } 
\qquad(4)
$$

---

### Lithospheric Flexure

The flexural response of the lithosphere is governed by:

$$
D(x,y) \nabla^{4} h + \lambda h = q(x,y) 
\qquad(5)
$$

With flexural rigidity:

$$
D(x,y) = \frac{E T_{e}(x,y)^{3}}{12(1 - \nu^{2})} 
\qquad(6)
$$

In Fourier domain:

$$
h(k) = - \frac{g(k) e^{|k|z_0} + F(k)}{C + D |k|^{4} + \lambda} 
\qquad(7)
$$

---

### Variable Elastic Thickness

The effective elastic thickness is modelled as:

$$
T_{e}(x,y) = T_{e0} \left( 1 + a \, h(x,y) \right) 
\qquad(8)
$$

---

### Adaptive Low-Pass Filtering

A cosine-tapered filter is applied in spectral domain:

$$
B(k) = 
\begin{cases}
1, & |k| \leq k_{\text{low}} \\
\frac{1}{2} \left[ 1 + \cos \left( \pi \frac{|k| - k_{\text{low}}}{k_{\text{high}} - k_{\text{low}}} \right) \right], & k_{\text{low}} < |k| < k_{\text{high}} \\
0, & |k| \geq k_{\text{high}}
\end{cases} 
\qquad(9)
$$

---

### Convergence Criterion

The misfit is defined as:

$$
\epsilon = \frac{\| g_{\text{obs}} - g_{\text{calc}} \|_2}{\| g_{\text{obs}} \|_2} 
\qquad(10)
$$

The iteration stops when $\epsilon < \epsilon_{0}$.

---

### Numerical Scheme

Each iteration consists of:

- Forward FFT of $g(x,y)$ and $h(x,y)$
- Spectral update of $h(k)$
- Nonlinear correction terms
- Adaptive filtering $B(k)$
- Inverse FFT to physical space
- Misfit evaluation

---

### Final Model

The estimated Moho depth is obtained as:

$$
h(x,y) = \mathcal{F}^{-1} { h(k) }
$$
